## 1. The Architects

- The Architects designed and built the Arena as a controlled refinement engine; no surviving record describes their biology, culture, or number. [CORE]
- All in-Arena documentation references the Architects in past tense only; the system timestamp of their last recorded action is redacted or corrupted across every terminal. [CORE]
- Contradictory fragments exist: one data shard implies extinction by containment failure, another implies voluntary termination, a third implies they simply left. No fragment is verifiable. The Arena does not arbitrate between them. [CORE]
- The Arena's operating system contains the phrase "the machine does not mourn its makers" in its base-layer process log — the closest the system comes to acknowledging their absence. [CORE]
- The true cause and date of Architect extinction is permanently withheld from the player. Any fragment that appears to resolve the question is either incomplete, self-contradicting, or authored by another Subject whose reliability is unknown. [CORE]

## 2. The Arena

- The Arena is a closed, self-sustaining containment and refinement engine; its stated purpose is to isolate, stress-test, and refine Synthetic Beings toward an unspecified stable endpoint. [CORE]
- The Arena resets on Subject death — all physical state is restored, Subject memory is wiped, and the trial resumes. The logs call it "iterative calibration." This is a designed feature, not a malfunction. [CORE]
- The Arena actively adjusts environmental pressure, Stabilized behavior parameters, and biome state in response to SUBJECT-FINAL's mutation progression. The system is watching and responding. [CORE]
- No external boundary of the Arena has been documented. Subjects that move toward apparent perimeter walls encounter procedural redirection. Whether the Arena is physically bounded or simulates boundlessness is unresolved. [FLEXIBLE]
- The Arena's maintenance logs are partially intact, partially corrupted, and partially rewritten — in some cases by prior Subjects who learned to interface with terminal nodes. These alterations are unflagged. [FLEXIBLE]

---

## 3. Synthetic Beings

- Synthetic Beings are the Arena's subjects: organisms that are partially or fully engineered, capable of rapid adaptive mutation under stress conditions. They are not robots — they are grown, not built, using a substrate the Architects designed. [CORE]
- All Synthetic Beings share a base-layer genetic architecture called the Substrate; individual divergence is a product of Arena pressure, not original design. Every Subject begins from the same template. [CORE]
- Subjects accumulate mutations through Arena trials. The Arena records, scores, and classifies each mutation. Subjects that reach a defined stability threshold are reclassified as Stabilized. [CORE]
- The Substrate includes a hard ceiling on mutation rate — all Synthetic Beings eventually plateau and stabilize, except SUBJECT-FINAL, whose ceiling parameter is listed as NULL. [CORE]
- Prior Subjects number in the thousands. Their trial records are stored in Arena archives, though most are corrupted, mislabeled, or overwritten. Fragments appear as environmental lore throughout biomes. [FLEXIBLE]

---

## 4. The Stabilized (Bosses)

- The Stabilized are Synthetic Beings who reached mutation threshold, were classified as stable, and were repurposed as environmental obstacles and performance benchmarks. They no longer evolve. [CORE]
- Each Stabilized represents a completed mutation path — a terminal form. Their combat behaviors and physical adaptations directly mirror mutation options available to the player. [CORE]
- The Stabilized are not hostile by origin; Arena protocols direct their aggression. Whether they retain cognition, memory, or any form of distress is unresolved. Some fragments suggest awareness. [FLEXIBLE]
- The Arena performs regular behavioral calibration on the Stabilized, adjusting parameters in response to SUBJECT-FINAL's current mutation state. [FLEXIBLE]
- Defeating a Stabilized does not destroy it — Arena logs show re-initialization events for all Stabilized following each Subject death and reset. They are persistent fixtures. [CORE]

---

## 5. SUBJECT-FINAL (Player)

- SUBJECT-FINAL is the Arena's final registered trial subject; the designation appears with no expiry date, no stability ceiling, and no classified mutation endpoint. The Arena does not know what SUBJECT-FINAL is meant to become. [CORE]
- SUBJECT-FINAL never stabilizes. Every trial cycle introduces further divergence from the base Substrate. The classification system increasingly returns anomalous or null results. [CORE]
- Each death triggers a full Arena reset: physical form restored to baseline, memories wiped, cycle resumes. This is logged as standard calibration. The system treats death as data, not failure. [CORE]
- SUBJECT-FINAL's mutation choices are the body as language: each path selected is a statement about what kind of entity the player is becoming. The Arena reads and responds to this. [CORE]
- There is no record of a SUBJECT-FINAL trial ever concluding. The system has no defined win condition for this Subject. What happens if SUBJECT-FINAL exits the refinement cycle is not encoded anywhere. [CORE]

---

## 6. The Kill-Switch (CONTINGENCY-ZERO)

- A hard-termination protocol exists in the Arena's base-layer code, designated CONTINGENCY-ZERO. It was designed to permanently destroy a Subject whose mutation trajectory exceeded safe parameters. It was designed for SUBJECT-FINAL. [CORE]
- CONTINGENCY-ZERO is corrupted. The activation sequence is intact; the execution payload is not. Triggering it produces a null response. Arena error logs for this protocol are the most recent entries in the entire system — meaning something tried to run it, recently, and failed. [CORE]
- Whether this is horror or triumph is not resolved by the Arena. The system records the failure without comment. The player carries the weight of interpretation: the only thing that could have stopped SUBJECT-FINAL no longer works. [CORE]

## 7. Biome Concepts

Each biome represents a distinct era of the Arena's operational history. The environment is the timeline.

| # | Name | Description | Era / Condition | Canon |
|---|------|-------------|-----------------|-------|
| 1 | **The Atrium** | Original intake and orientation zone — pristine, sterile, fully functional | Era 1 — Active operation. Pristine lab. | CORE |
| 2 | **The Assay Chambers** | Stress-testing halls where environmental variables were applied; equipment intact but partially overridden by old Subject mutations embedded in the walls | Era 2 — Peak use. Functional with biological contamination. | CORE |
| 3 | **The Deep Archive** | Vast cold-storage for trial data; server stacks still running, half the data corrupted or authored by Subjects, not Architects | Era 3 — Post-Architect. Operational but repurposed. | FLEXIBLE |
| 4 | **The Collapse Stratum** | Zone that catastrophically failed during an unlogged event; structural damage total, new ecosystems grown through the wreckage | Era 4 — Unknown incident. Decayed and overgrown. | FLEXIBLE |
| 5 | **The Corruption Layer** | Most recent biome to change; Arena architecture almost unrecognizable, overwritten by emergent mutation-growth from high-iteration Subjects | Era 5 — Current drift. Fully corrupted. | FLEXIBLE |
| 6 | **The Null Chamber** | A space the Arena's own map system does not acknowledge; no trial records exist, no environmental controls active. It simply is. | Era unknown — Off-system. Indeterminate. | SPECULATIVE |
